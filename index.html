Selfie

We finally got to meet up. Alex had to stay two weeks in quarantine after arriving from France. In real social distancing fashion, we walked together slowly on the outskirts of Oslo. Walking on the fresh, soft forest grass, we caught up on our lives. Andreas is on his last year of uni and Alex is still doing her creative art stuff.

Our group selfie, angled towards the bright blue sky.

Game night

We decided not to go to the beach that one Saturday since it started to rain, even though we’d all been talking about going all summer. I couldn’t be more glad, honestly. Staying up all night playing Mario Kart was probably the most fun we had all summer. I was surprised at how determined Alex was to win. She only played as Donkey Kong.

Piglet

I got a snap from Andreas at his farm the other day. Mama pig had fourteen beautiful babies. This runt is one of Andreas’ favorite piglets. He’s just six-seven hours old in this picture. They’re all doing pretty well. When we stayed in Bygdøy he said I should come visit his farm and meet his sheep sometime. He lives on the other side of the country though and I (embarrassingly) don’t have my driver’s license yet, but he said he’d pick me up and we’d sing along to ABBA on the whole ten hour long drive.

Beach

You're not here anymore, so I had to go alone. I messaged Alex, but I'm not getting any replies. I hope something serious didn't happen, and that she's doing okay. Every now and then I think about us, and ask myself the same questions. Maybe we spent too much time doing the same things over and over, and maybe you weren't really enjoying yourself that much? I wish I'd spent more time getting to know you when I had the chance, and that we had more of the serious conversations. The truth is probably just that you're busy living your lives just like I'm living mine, but I find it difficult trying to remain rational, not knowing where you are. At least I'm grateful we got to hang out at all, and I hope you know that you can still reach out to me whenever.
